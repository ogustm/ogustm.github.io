History

Barcelona was founded in 1638 as Nueva Barcelona del Cerro Santo by the Spanish conqueror of Catalan origin Joan Orpí. In 1671 it was refounded by Governor Sancho Fernández de Angulo two kilometres south of the original site. Barcelona was one of the provinces under the authority of the government of New Andalusia. It was also called the province of Nueva Barcelona. In 1761, the province was bordered to the north by the town of Pozuelos; to the west by the river Unare up to its head; to the east by the table of Guanipa and to the south by the river Orinoco.
